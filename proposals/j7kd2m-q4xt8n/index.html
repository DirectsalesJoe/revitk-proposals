No visibility on how many prospects are at each stage, where they're stuck, or what's needed to move them. Aaron, you said it yourself: you're going on gut feel, and by the time you realise someone's not performing, months have passed.

"I've done so much over the years and none of it's worked. I've just wasted an absolute load of money."

No call structure. No way to assess whether they're any good until months of salary have gone. You've spent thousands on salespeople who didn't work out, and the root cause was the same every time: there was no process for them to follow.

Keep doing what you're doing

Chloe's doing good work. She's generating conversations, connecting on LinkedIn, getting meetings. But without a structured sales process, a follow-up cadence, and a mechanism to convert signed-up suppliers into active accounts, the conversion rate stays where it is. Another quarter passes. Another 20 accounts sit dormant. That's another £200K left on the table.

Buy a tool or a database

You've tried data before. Delight is a step in the right direction for dormant client reactivation. But tools without a process are like giving someone a sat nav without telling them where they're going. The CRM is being used as a contact list, not a pipeline management tool.

Build the system

Map out every stage of your sales process. Give Chloe (and anyone who comes after her) a framework that works whether Aaron's involved or not. Get the data for the decision-makers inside your existing accounts. Build the follow-up cadences. Structure the onboarding process so that supplier setup becomes first order, not supplier setup becomes dormant account. And give yourself visibility over what's happening without having to sit on every call or chase every lead.

One of these solves the root cause. The other three treat symptoms.

05 · Approach

How We Do It

Build the track before you ask people to run on it.

Most businesses try to fix sales by training the salesperson. Send them on a course. Get them to read a book. Maybe bring in a consultant for a day.

It doesn't work because the salesperson isn't the problem. The system is the problem. If there's no process, no playbook, and no feedback loop, then training is just giving someone better shoes to run on a broken track.

What we do instead: build the track first.

The Sync Selling System reverse-engineers your sales process from your actual deals. We look at how your buyers decide, what your best clients said before they came on board, and where deals stall or die. Then we build a step-by-step process around that reality.

Phase 01 · Diagnose (Weeks 1-2)

We've already started this. The workshop gave us the raw material. Now we go deeper. Full diagnostic of every stage of your sales process. Every leak identified. Every opportunity ranked by impact and ease of implementation. The output is a complete GTM Diagnostic Report with a prioritised roadmap.

For United Drains, the priority sequence is clear:

  1. Dormant account activation (the 20 accounts, the existing groups)
  2. Onboarding and review cadence (supplier setup to first order)
  3. Discovery framework (finding the pain, not just the logistics)
  4. Quote follow-up process (surveyors calling, automated sequences)
  5. CRM pipeline setup and automation (or advice on moving to something simpler)
  6. Outreach playbook for new business (LinkedIn, cold call, email)
  7. Hiring playbook for future sales hires

Phase 02 · Build (Weeks 2-6)

Every script, every email template, every meeting framework, every follow-up sequence. Tailored to United Drains. Tailored to the different personas: FM companies, hotel groups, pub chains, direct corporates.

This includes the Sync Selling AI Sales Toolkit, built on your specific business. Chloe drops a call transcript in, and within minutes she gets a scored review of how the call went, a follow-up email ready to send, and coaching on what to ask next time. You see at a glance how she's performing and whether the process is being followed. No more black box.

Phase 03 · Coach (Weeks 6-12)

We train Chloe on every playbook. Walk her through the discovery framework so she's asking the questions that open up dissatisfaction. Role-play the onboarding conversation so the "would you have any objection to testing us with a couple of small jobs?" feels natural. Review her live deals and give feedback on what's working and what needs adjusting.

You attend group coaching calls. Bring deals. Ask questions. See how other founders are tackling the same problems. The AI dashboard tracks progress week over week, so you can see the improvement in real numbers.
