10 · FAQs

Questions You Might Be Asking

Our product is already converting well. Why do I need a sales process?

Because 20–40% means 60–80% are saying no. Your product is strong enough to convert buyers who figure out the business case on their own. A sales process converts the ones who need help getting there. That's the gap between £10M and what comes next.

I adjust my demo on the fly for different buyer types. Can a playbook really capture that?

That's exactly what it captures. We watch how you adjust, document the decision points, and create branching frameworks so anyone following the process makes the same reads you do. The playbook doesn't replace your instinct. It codifies it.

Charles doesn't feel confident doing demos. Will this help?

Charles doesn't lack confidence. He lacks a structured demo flow. When the demo is "watch Dagny and try to replicate," that's intimidating. When it's a step-by-step framework with specific questions and pivot points, it's followable. The process does the heavy lifting, not personality.

Will AI tools really work for our niche?

The AI toolkit is built on YOUR process, YOUR buyer personas, YOUR objection patterns. It's not generic. It scores calls against your specific playbook, writes emails using your positioning, and coaches against the framework we build together. Generic AI gives generic answers. This doesn't.

Cultural and gender dynamics exist. They're real. But they're not why deals stall between demo and decision. Deals stall because the prospect hasn't built a business case, there's no committed next step, and the follow-up doesn't give them a reason to re-engage. The process fixes the system.
